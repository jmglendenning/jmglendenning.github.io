Article Review

article
review
Author

John Glendenning

Published

October 4, 2025

This is my standard routine for doing a literature search, which has been used in the past. This review for methodologies, not for studies like health outcomes, some analysis is skipped.

  • Define the question and limit the scope In this case, How has this thing been used in this area for this function?

  • Initial search strategy, web & primarily library

    • Keywords
    • Vary keyword combinations
    • Download files

  • Selection criteria

    • Include: Reviewed articles, journal articles, primarily English, biased to social sciences, book chapters
    • Exclude: Opinions, reviews, editorials, dupes, anything not empirical

  • Screening

    After downloading screen the documents based on

    • Title
    • Abstract
    • Conclusion

    Appropriate articles moved into screened pile. Put into keep or hold piles.

  • References

    Quickly review the references of the keep documents based on:

    • Repeated references
    • Interesting titles
    • Known authors

    Add to download pile and loop back to screening

  • Cites

    Create a bibiliography of screened files. Can be run through an LLM to review the number of citations for the kept documents. More important for documents that are older. Useless for recently released publications.

  • Quality

    • Skim document to assess quality and applicability
    • Assess group trends
    • Identify gaps
    • Rebin the articles into keep or hold

  • Read

    Read the selected articles from the keep pile.
